# Israeli Success Story: Monday.com's Road from Underdog to SaaS Giant

**Author:** Sophie Shulman
**Publication Date:** November 24, 2024
**Source:** CTech

## Key Statistics & Growth Metrics

- **Annual Recurring Revenue:** $1 billion (announced end of summer 2024)
- **Q3 Growth:** Nearly 30%
- **Current Market Valuation:** Approximately $13.8 billion
- **Stock Performance Since IPO (2021):** Nearly doubled in value
- **Market Position:** Among five largest Israeli companies by market capitalization
- **Expected Year-End Revenues:** Approximately $970 million
- **Operating Profit:** $100 million
- **Cash Flow:** $250 million
- **Gross Margin:** ~90% of revenue
- **Customer Retention Rate:** Consistently exceeded 100% since IPO
- **Current Employees:** Nearly 2,500 (1,100 in Israel)
- **Employee Growth:** 600 (5 years ago) → 800 (IPO, 3 years ago) → 2,500 (present)
- **Global Workforce Distribution:** 55% outside Israel
- **CRM Market Position:** Fourth place for medium-sized organizations (within one year)

## Founder Ownership Stakes

- **Roy Mann:** 11.2% stake (~$1.5 billion value)
- **Eran Zinman:** 4.4% stake (~$600 million value)

## Notable Quotes

**On company maturity:** "We see the company as being in its adolescence...we believe we can grow tenfold."

**On future ambitions:** "We're updating that goal to $10 billion. Competitors have reached $10 billion, and we believe we have everything needed."

**On AI:** "For us, AI is not a threat but a boost...monday paired with AI is a powerful combination."

**On founder leadership:** "Investors now prefer founders to remain at the helm."

## Company Background

- **Founded:** 2012
- **IPO:** 2021
- **Original Name:** dapulse (changed in 2016)
- **Original Revenue at Name Change:** $20 million
- **Founders' Background:** Met in IDF Unit 9900 (Intelligence Corps)
- **Initial Operations:** Based in Wix office with Wix as first customer

## Market Position & Competitive Advantages

The company transformed from being dismissed as a "colorful Excel" by investors to establishing itself as a comprehensive workplace software platform. Key differentiators include user-friendly design, intuitive interface, and organic expansion within customer organizations rather than top-down enterprise implementation.
